Faculty

Dr Ram Krishna

Dr Ram Krishna is an experienced metals researcher and academic with a background in metallurgy. He received his Bachelor of Engineering degree in metallurgical engineering and material science from the National Institute of Technology (NIT), Jamshedpur, and his Master's degree in materials and metallurgical engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur. Dr Krishna continued his studies at the University of Leicester, UK, obtaining his PhD in 2011. He has held postdoctoral positions at the University of Manchester, UK and North Carolina State University, USA. His research interests cover many materials for high-temperature applications, including mechanical testing of their behaviour and performance and advanced characterisation techniques, as evidenced by his H-index of 14 and 2 utility patents under examination. He has authored or co-authored more than 52 research papers in international journals and conference proceedings. His current research focuses on 3D printing of metallic and ceramic alloys and materials for 3D printing applications.

Dr Binay Kumar

Dr. Binay Kumar is currently working as an Assistant professor in the Department of Metallurgical and Materials Engineering, National Institute of Technology (NIT) Jamshedpur, Jharkhand, India since Nov. 2022. He completed his M.Tech. and Ph.D. from the Department of Metallurgical and Materials Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, in the years 2015 and 2021, respectively. Before joining NIT Jamshedpur, he was working in Research and Development Division of ArcelorMittal Nippon Steel (AMNS) India, Surat, Gujarat. He has also worked in the Foundry Division of Tata Motors Ltd., Jamshedpur before enrolling at IIT Kharagpur for his M.Tech. course. His major area of research interest includes alternative routes of ironmaking and steelmaking, process optimization, exergy analysis, energy and emission control, and thermodynamic modelling. Apart from this, he is also well-versed with the application of thermodynamic software like Thermo-Calc and FactSage in his research field. He is a member of the Indian Institute of Metals, Kolkata, India.
